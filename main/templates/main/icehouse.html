  • About the Ice House

The “Old Ice House” now the home of California Wine Classics and Serendipity Sparkling Wine Cellars, was originally the hub for Lodi area produce shipped to local and long distance destinations. Ice, frozen on-site, would be stored in 300-pound cans or cut into large blocks. Once loaded onto railcars alongside produce such a Tokay grapes, farmers and distributors counted on the ice to keep produce fresh until it arrived at its destination, sometimes to locations as far as Chicago or New York. Estimated to have been built in the late 1880s, the Old Ice House saw a number of occupants before it was converted into a winery in 2011. After Lodi’s commerce shifted from fresh produce to wine, Birdseye used the building as a packing center for frozen food. Music jam sessions, after hour parties, an auto mechanic warehouse are just some of the uses that flowed in an out of 27 Locust Street over the years! During renovation, a lot was learned about the structure itself. To keep the space cool, the outer walls are over 12 inches thick with a core of cork oak. The inner walls, aiming to insulate individual rooms, were filled with packed redwood sawdust. Though renovations were costly and time consuming, its previous life in the ice business made the conversion into a climate controlled wine production and storage facility ideal. With renovations complete, the Old Ice House is now home to the California Wine Classics and Serendipity Sparkling Wine Cellars. http://www.lodinews.com/news/article_ede9f45b-d4a5-5972-a8dc-040bd978e8cf.html
